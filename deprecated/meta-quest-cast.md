## **8. Casting from Meta Quest**

Casting allows you to display what the user sees inside the headset on another device such as a phone, computer, or TV.
Before starting, make sure that **all devices are connected to the same Wi-Fi network** and, if applicable, that they are **logged into the same Meta account**.

---

### **8.1 Cast to a Phone**

**Requirements**

* The **Meta Horizon** app installed on your phone.
* Your **phone** and **Meta Quest** headset on the **same Wi-Fi** network.
* **Bluetooth** enabled on your phone.

**Steps**

1. Open the **Meta Horizon** app on your phone.
2. Tap **Menu → Devices → Cast.**
3. Select your **Meta Quest** headset from the list.
4. Tap **Start Casting** to begin.
5. The phone will now display the headset view in real time.

**To stop casting**

* Tap the casting preview on your phone → **X → Stop Casting.**
* Casting also stops automatically if you close the Meta app.

---

### **8.2 Cast to a Computer**

**Requirements**

* A computer on the **same Wi-Fi network** as the headset.
* A modern web browser (Chrome, Edge, Firefox).
* A Meta account.

**Steps**

1. On your computer, open a browser and go to **[oculus.com/casting](https://oculus.com/casting)**.
2. Log in with your **Meta account**.
3. Put on your **Meta Quest** headset.
4. Press the **Meta (Oculus)** button on the right controller to open the **Universal Menu**.
5. Select the **clock** on the left side to open **Quick Settings**.
6. Select **Cast → Web**, then choose **Computer**.
7. Confirm by selecting **Next**.
8. The browser page will now show the headset display.

**To stop casting**

* Open **Quick Settings → Cast → Stop Casting** in your headset.

---

### **8.3 Cast to a TV using Google Chromecast**

**Requirements**

* A **Google Chromecast** device (or a smart TV with Chromecast built-in).
* The **Google Home** app installed on your phone.
* All devices on the **same Wi-Fi network**.

#### **1. Set up the Chromecast**

1. Plug the Chromecast into an **HDMI port** on the TV.
2. Connect the Chromecast’s **USB power cable** (either to the TV’s USB port or a wall outlet).
3. Turn on the TV and select the **HDMI input** where the Chromecast is connected.
4. Open the **Google Home** app on your phone.
5. Tap **“+ → Set up device → New device”** and follow the instructions:

   * Allow Bluetooth and location access.
   * Confirm the code shown on the TV.
   * Connect Chromecast to your **Wi-Fi** network.
6. Wait for the device to update and complete setup.
7. Once done, your TV will show the Chromecast home screen.

#### **2. Cast from the Meta Quest headset**

1. Put on your **Meta Quest 2 or 3** headset.
2. Press the **Meta (Oculus)** button on the right controller to open the **Universal Menu**.
3. Select the **clock** on the left-hand side to open **Quick Settings**.
4. Tap **Cast**.
5. In the list of devices, select your **Chromecast or TV**.
6. Select **Next**, and the TV will begin showing the headset display.

**To stop casting**

* Open **Quick Settings → Cast → Stop Casting.**

---

### **Tips**

* If casting doesn’t appear, verify all devices are on the **same Wi-Fi network and frequency band** (2.4GHz or 5GHz).
* Restart your **headset**, **Chromecast**, or **router** if the device list doesn’t update.
* For best performance, keep all devices close to the Wi-Fi router.
* You can also cast to the **Meta Quest Link app** on PC if it’s connected to the headset.
